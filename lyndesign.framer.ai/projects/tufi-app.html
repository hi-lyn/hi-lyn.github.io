UX Case Study | Tutor Finder App

🚀 Welcome to Tutor Finder - The Tutor Booking and Study Progress Tracking App for Parents and Children!

About Project

Tutor Finder is designed to meet the growing demand for education and child development in today's society, especially within the Asian parenting community. We understand that finding the right tutor is not only a challenge but also a time-consuming process, often plagued by issues such as high costs, inefficiencies in existing apps, and uncertainties regarding the availability of local tutors.

With Tutor Finder, we commit to providing parents and children with an easy, convenient, and effective experience. Integrating features for scheduling and tracking study progress, the app alleviates the burden on parents in finding an ideal tutor while scientifically monitoring the development of their children.

🌟 Why Choose Tutor Finder?

  • 🧭 Easy Discovery: Explore a diverse list of tutors with convenience and speed.

  • 📆 Flexible Scheduling: Schedule lessons at your convenience, aligning with your and the tutor's availability.

  • 📊 Track Progress: Review detailed and scientific insights into your child's academic development.

Validating the Problem

In the realm of education, the challenge persists for students and parents alike in finding the right tutor – someone professional, available, and in sync with the student's schedule. Additionally, the demands on Asian parents are escalating as the traditional practice of shuttling kids to multiple after-school classes takes a toll on time, health, and security concerns. The Tutor Finder App addresses this conundrum, streamlining the search for tutors and revolutionizing supplementary education by focusing on professionalism, availability, and the evolving dynamics of contemporary parenting.

Problem:

  1. Personalization Gap: Current platforms lack personalized learning experiences, hindering tailored education for individual students.

  2. Communication Hurdles: Ineffective communication with tutors creates challenges in setting clear expectations and goals for tutoring sessions.

  3. Pricing Ambiguity: Ambiguous tutor fees on existing platforms cause user frustration, emphasizing the need for transparent pricing.

  4. Limited Specialization: Users struggle to find tutors with specialized knowledge, limiting options for comprehensive learning in niche subjects.

  5. Progress Tracking Issues: Existing platforms lack effective tools for monitoring academic progress, leaving users uncertain about the effectiveness of tutoring sessions.

Solution

  1. Personalized Learning: Utilize advanced algorithms for tailored tutoring based on individual needs and learning styles.

  2. Efficient Communication: Provide a user-friendly platform for clear communication, fostering expectations and goal-setting.

  3. Transparent Pricing: Ensure clarity in tutor fees with a transparent pricing model for various services and durations.

  4. Subject Specialization: Enhance search capabilities to find tutors with specialized knowledge in niche subjects.

  5. Progress Tracking Tools: Integrate robust tools for real-time monitoring and assessment of academic progress.

Design Process

User-centered Design (UCD) is an approach that prioritizes the needs, goals, and preferences of the end-users throughout the design process. It involves the following iterative steps:

User Research

  • Students often struggle with understanding complex concepts and need personalized support.

  • Parents rely on word-of-mouth or online reviews when selecting tutors.

  • Features such as tutor ratings, subject expertise, and interactive learning tools are crucial for an effective tutoring platform.

  • Safety, credibility, and effective communication are primary concerns for parents.

  • Students and parents appreciate the flexibility of both online and offline tutoring options.

  • The tutor's teaching style and ability to adapt to individual learning needs are key factors in the selection process.

Questions I Asked

  1. What challenges do students face in their academic journey?

  2. How do parents currently search for and select tutors for their children?

  3. What features would make an online tutoring platform appealing to both students and parents?

  4. What concerns do parents have regarding the safety and effectiveness of online tutoring?

  5. How do students prefer to engage with tutors - online, offline, or a combination of both?

  6. What criteria are important for both students and parents when choosing a tutor?

Quantitative research

User Personas

A persona within user-centered design is an imaginary character crafted to embody a user type anticipated to interact with a site, brand, or product in a comparable manner.

Empathy Map

An empathy map serves as a collaborative tool for teams, facilitating a deeper understanding of their customers. Similar to a user persona, this tool can collectively represent a group of users, such as a specific customer segment.

Pain Point
- Limited time to search for suitable tutors
- Concerns about finding tutors who can act as positive female role models
- Ensuring her daughters feel comfortable and supported during tutoring sessions

Task Mapping

User Flow:

User flow outlines how a user navigates through a product's designed templates to achieve their goal, predicting and illustrating potential interaction routes.3 / 3

Card sorting

In card sorting, users work with predetermined category names, organizing individual cards into these predefined categories. This method doesn't unveil users' conceptualization of topics but serves to assess how effectively an existing category structure aligns with user perspectives on content.

Wireframe

Utilizing user flow, sitemaps, and spacing principles, I iteratively crafted wireframes. The objective was to ensure clarity of information and ease of navigation.

All screen

Usability Testing

Usability testing, a pivotal practice in user-centered interaction design, involves evaluating a product by observing its use among real users. It provides invaluable direct insights into user interactions with the system.

Positive Feedback: The app concept, UX, and UI garnered favorable feedback.

Enhancement Opportunities: Suggestions include expanding payment options and incorporating email-based login for user convenience.

Overall Practicality: Users found the application practical with a commendable UI.

The usability testing involved 3 users, resulting in mostly positive outcomes. Minor enhancements identified during the testing phase will be implemented for continuous improvement.

Project learning

Understanding our users, especially parents, is paramount for the success of our Tutor Finder App. Simplicity and clarity are key in gaining their trust, as it allows information to be effortlessly processed. While staying aware of visual design trends, prioritizing the needs and usability of our target users ensures significant value for both parents and the company in the initial stages.

Recognizing the transient nature of ideas, we emphasize the importance of daily practice to enhance our learning and processing capabilities. Iteration is at the core of our approach, enabling us to continually refine and improve the layout and functionality of each app component. This iterative process is fundamental in adapting to evolving user needs and ensuring the sustained success of the Tutor Finder App.
